wip
Combat is structured in Rounds. During each Round each Character plays his Combat#Turn in the order determined by the Combat#Initiative Roll.
Player Characters can always choose to act later than their Combat#Initiative

Initiative

wip
Initiative x 2 + Dice Roll. Highest goes first.

Turn

During their turns, characters spend Actions to do shit. They have 1 Main Action and 1 Side Action per Turn

Main Actions

A Main Action is something impactful (such as hitting an enemy, making a grand monologue, setting up a trap, charging up a devastating attack). All character should get their chances to shine, that's why the GM should think of these in terms of impact and not in term of length. and that's why some Main Actions might take longer than others.

Side Actions

Side Actions are all the little things a character might need to do during the fight but are not impactful enough to warrant spending their whole turn doing it.

Free Actions

It's free real estate. Let your players have fun. Anything that does not impact the numbers should go in here (lighting a cigarette, shouting the name of your technique)

Combat Characteristics

At character creation, Player Character choose for each of their Combat Characteristics a different Characteristics.
These characteristics are only used in combat and are kind of a shorthand so that player don't have to remember the choice they made at creation.

Damage

How much damage you will inflict with each successful hit.
Value equal to the chosen Characteristic.

Precision

Added to your combat skill for offensive maneuvers, such as applying your fist to someone's face.
Value equal to the chosen Characteristic.

Defense

Added to your combat skill for defensive maneuvers, such as dodging or blocking incoming attacks
Value equal to the chosen Characteristic.

Fortitude

How much of a beating you can actually take before falling down.
See Hit Points for conversion.
example
Why won't you die?

  • Wis : Years of battle has tempered you.
  • Brain : By sheer force of will you drag your body around even with impressive scars
  • Aura : You're just that awesome
  • Dex : Minute movements allow you to minimize the damage your receive
  • Instincts : Minute movements allow you to minimize the damage your receive, you don't even have to think about it nowadays
  • Body : You're a rock, hardened through battle and effort.

Strain

How many times you can Strain yourself. Strain is used to make more actions in a round and for certain abilities.
Value equal to the chosen Characteristic.

Initiative

How quickly you act during a Turn.
Value equal to the chosen Characteristic.

Hit Points

Determines of much damage a character can take before falling in combat. The general state of the character is separated in 4 Tiers : Healthy, Bruised, Wounded, Death's Door.
Characters, unless otherwise specified start at the Healthy Threshold.
When they progress to a new threshold (see Receiving Damage) they gain bonuses in combat. Threshold Bonuses are cumulative, meaning that a Character receives the bonuses from all previous Thresholds.
Outside of Combat they suffer a Malus instead

Threshold Bonus Malus
Healthy None None
Bruised +1 Quick per Turn None
Wounded +2 to all Rolls -2 to all Rolls
Death's Door Limit Break Unconscious

Receiving Damage

When a character receives Damage, they check the Damage Value against their current HP Threshold.
If the Damage is superior, subtract the Threshold Value to the Damage Value.
The Character enters the next threshold.
Repeat with the updated Threshold and Damage Value.
When the Damage is under the Threshold Value, the character is unaffected. In terms of narration, they shrug off the blow or they are already so beat up that the damage is only marginal

Death's Door

The threshold for Death's door is slightly different. Each time you receive an attack, make a Toughness Roll. This becomes your Threshold Value for the Attack. The Damage Value used to compare if the character has died is the full damage of the attack before any reduction.
remark
When a character would die, he does so at the end of the current character's turn.

Strain

Think of it as mental/physical fatigue. Used as a cost for certain actions. If a character has no Strain left and tries to perform an action that requires spending Strain, he automatically progress to the next Damage Threshold a number of times equal to the Strain he should have spent.

Combat Specific Actions

Movement

For a Side Action cover a Short distance (roughly 12 units)
For a Main Action cover a Long distance (roughly 36 units)
You can take other Actions in the middle of this movement.

Use a Fighting Style's Spell

Usually a Main Action. Check description of the Fighting Style#Spell.

Use a Fighting Style Technique

See Soul Forging.

Attack of Opportunity

If your Strike is still capable of targeting in Melee Range, whenever a Character exits your Melee Range without using the Combat#Disengage Action, you can perform an Attack of Opportunity.
For this, choose a Fighting Style and make a Precision + Fighting Style Roll, on success follow the usual Combat#Resolving Attacks

Action Cost Target Range Dice Roll On success Additional Effect
Free Single Melee Precision + Fighting Style Damage + Dice None

Disengage

As a Main Action, perform a Short Move without triggering Combat#Attack of Opportunity

Grapple

Action Cost Target Range Dice Roll On success Additional Effect
Main Single Melee Precision + Grappling Target and Caster are Conditions#Grappled None

See Conditions#Grappled. Name is maybe misleading, it's more a hands locked kinda thing than two people wrestling on the ground.

Exerting

As a Free Action a character can Exert himself. Gaining an additional Side Action during his turn. Each time a character Exerts himself he suffers 1 Strain

Going All-Out

When performing an Action in Combat, a Character may go All-out. If they are not already Guard Broken, they may transform their Dice Roll into a Risky Roll with consequences : Apply Conditions#Break to self.
Modulation Characteristic should be Defense (in most cases).
(Remember that this will not result in an increase in Damage as the Modulation does not impact the Dice but the total)

Resolving Attacks

Attack Step

Player Characters

When a Player character wants to attack an NPC :
First they select an attack for which they still have the necessary actions for.
Then they select a target (or a target area if your attack is an AoE).
Then make a Precision + Relevant Skill Dice Roll. This determines the Attack Value of the Attack. All targets, or characters in the target area, whose Defense is lower than the roll suffer the effects of the attack

Non-Player Characters

When an NPC attacks, they do not roll any dice. The GM designs a character that his NPC attacks. The NPC does not roll for the attack. Instead he uses his Attack Value.
Proceeds onto the Defense step

Defense Step

Player Characters

When a Player Character is attacked. Make a Defense + Relevant Skill Dice Roll.
If the Result is above the Attack Value, then the character has completely dodged the attack.
Otherwise proceed onto the Damage Step

Non-Player Characters

When an NPC character is attacked instead, compare his Defense Value with the Atttack Value of the attack. If it is above the Attack Value, then the character has completely dodged the attack.
Otherwise proceed onto the Damage Step

Damage Step

Player Characters is attacked

If a player character is hit by an attack. Reduce the Damage Value by the Dice Result (not the roll, only the dice). This is the damage they take

Player Characters attacks

If a PC's attack lands. They inflict damage to the NPC equal to their Damage + Dice Result (once again only the value on the dice, not the entire roll).

Auto-Crits

Re-roll dice as if you had made a critical Hit, with value indicated on you dice. follow standard procedure for critical hits
/ Change for add 9 to the roll
