Contrary to Spells, Techniques from Fighting Style depend on your Strike.
By default, Strike applies the default of the Technique's Fighting Style, and on a successful hit, will deal damage.
Soul Forging passives are unlocked when leveling up a Fighting Style and modifies the properties of your Strike and thus your techniques.

A Default Strike or technique could summarized as such.

Action Cost Target Range Affected Dice Roll All the time On success Additional Effect
Main Single Melee Target Precision + Fighting Style Apply Default Damage + Dice None

Techniques might apply some modifiers (e.g. Target all in short range, Strike Twice...) but these apply only to this specific Technique.

Soul Forging Passives change the default properties of your Strike across all Techniques and Spells.

Targeting

A single Character may only be targeted once.
You may not target anything else than a Character, unless otherwise specified.

Soul Forging Slots

Most Soul Forging Passives require Slots to be equipped. These slots usually come from gear, or from Restrictions that the character take to pay for the slots.
(Note that some Restrictions actually require Slots and some Soul Forging#Soul Forging Passives grant some)

Restrictions

Power comes with a price, and strong weapons usually come with limitations. These restrictions provide modifiers to the total power level of weapons

Ammunition

Soul Forging Slots vary depending on the amount of shots you can perform before needing to reload and the time it takes to reload.

Charge

Before attacking with this weapon, the user must perform a Charge Action beforehand without being interrupted. If the character performs any other actions, he loses the benefits of the charge. Targets have to be declared when you start charging. If you target an Area, or target moves out of the Range, the attack automatically misses.
The Charge Action's Cost depends on the weapon, and offer different modifier

Summon

Whenever you would use a Technique, you can do so with your summon as the caster. In that case, you must use the Characteristics and Skills of the Summon.
Whenever your Summon doesn't act during a turn, it follows the last instruction given, follow NPC rules for Damage and acts at the same Initiative as you.

Trap

When you perform techniques, store the effect in a trap at the targets location.
Traps cannot activate until the end of your turn.
You can have a maximum of 3 Traps on the field.
If there is a requirement for the target, choose he closest satisfying the condition. If there are none available, the trap doesn't activate.

Sustained

Whenever, you don't target ALL in an area, you can decide to Sustain your Technique.
While Sustained, the Technique continuously deals damage to any Character that enters in the area affected by your technique.
Sustaining cost a Main Action each turn.
While sustained, Techniques can be Short Moved for a Free Action (once per turn).
